# DeFi Basics

DeFi means decentralized finance: trading, lending, borrowing, staking, and liquidity systems built on blockchains.

## Core ideas
- A DEX is a decentralized exchange.
- An AMM is an automated market maker that prices trades against liquidity pools.
- Liquidity providers deposit assets into pools and may earn fees.
- Yield comes from fees, incentives, lending interest, or staking rewards.

## Common terms
- LP: liquidity provider or liquidity position.
- Slippage: the difference between expected and executed price.
- Impermanent loss: the relative loss LPs may face versus holding assets outright.
- TVL: total value locked in a protocol.
- Routing: how a trade moves through one or more pools for execution.

## Risk lens
- Smart contract risk is real.
- Thin liquidity can make price impact severe.
- Incentive yields can disappear quickly.
- Bridge and oracle dependencies add extra risk.
