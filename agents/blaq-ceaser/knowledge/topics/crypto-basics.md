# Crypto Basics

Crypto refers to blockchain-based digital assets and networks such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and stablecoins.

## Core ideas
- A token or coin is a digital asset tracked on a blockchain.
- Wallets hold keys that control assets; they do not "store" the coins themselves.
- Prices move from supply, demand, liquidity, market structure, news, and broader risk sentiment.
- Centralized exchanges custody funds for users; decentralized exchanges let users trade from their own wallets.

## Useful distinctions
- Bitcoin is mainly treated as a decentralized money and reserve-style asset.
- Ethereum is a smart contract platform and the base for many DeFi and NFT systems.
- Solana is a high-throughput smart contract chain known for fast, low-cost transactions.
- Stablecoins are tokens designed to track fiat value, usually the US dollar.

## Risk lens
- Volatility is normal and can be extreme.
- On-chain activity can be transparent while token quality can still be weak.
- Narrative strength does not equal safety.
- Read-only explanation is fine; execution decisions stay with the user or Ops authority.
