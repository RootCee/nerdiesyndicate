# NFT Basics

NFT stands for non-fungible token. NFTs usually represent unique digital items, membership access, collectibles, art, or in-game assets.

## Core ideas
- NFTs are unique tokens, unlike interchangeable fungible tokens.
- Value often depends on community, utility, rarity, culture, and liquidity.
- Collections usually have a floor price, while individual items may trade higher or lower.

## Common terms
- Mint: the first creation or release of NFTs from a collection.
- Floor price: the lowest listed price in a collection.
- Royalties: creator payments that may apply on secondary sales depending on market structure.
- Metadata: the descriptive data that defines the NFT item.

## Risk lens
- NFT markets can be highly illiquid.
- Floor prices can gap down fast.
- Hype cycles can distort price discovery.
- Authenticity, contract address checks, and scam avoidance matter.
